B2C Self-assistance solutions for Home Telco's services

Description

  • Orange France

  • 22 September 2020 to 31 July 2021

Orange provides Telecommunication services to millions of users in France, in addition to other worldwide countries. The project's business landscape is customer care, the purpose is to improve customer service and satisfaction, while controlling related costs. The main goal is to reduce overall number of interactions with human agents, by automating the resolution of telecommunication service problems/interruptions, besides reducing inaccurate hardware replacements that costs between 800K€ and 1M€, and last but not least, improve NPS (net promoter score). Within Orange Services factory, my role is to develop and stabilize the digital assistance solution for Orange public customers, in DevOps/SRE mode, with an end-to-end vision of the solution architecture.

Main responsibilities

  • Design target architecture taking into consideration interactions with legacy systems and their impact
  • Planned solution performance and load testing execution in collaboration with system administrator
  • Ensured that the solution is reliable in production using load balancing and circuit breaker
  • Led cross-functional teams internaly and external developement team to converge to the same project vision
  • Communicated release notes internally to all solution consumers
  • Planned developpement sprints in collaboration with consumer teams using scrum multi-teams methodology
  • Planned roll-out in production based on business owner roadmap and internal teams targeting 2 Millions users
  • Collaborated with designers to implement self-care user journey in the mobile application
  • Collaborated with business owner to monitor KPI related to customer satisfaction

Business impact

  • Decrease in after-sales costs: call-center phone calls' rate for troubleshooting HomeLAN services.
  • Improve customer assitance's quality through digital channels (self-assistance with mobile app, assistance with agent via web app), measured by surveys.
  • Reducing costs related to innacurate customer homeLAN services' diagnosis (costs evaluated at 800K euros, related to livebox given by Orange to customers following a complaint)
