Ride-sharing service - G7 cab drivers' applications

Description

  • G7 Taxis

  • November 2019, August 2020

I started this mission while G7 was in the midst of an important reorganisation, in fact, G7 was facing stiff competition from VTCs especially Uber in large public customers' segment, in consequence, G7 studied the situation, and came out with the conclusion that Uber are definetly winning in transactions' volume, but not necessarily with the service quality that a customer would expect.

In consequence, the business strategy turned to ensure high-quality service, and target professional customers (b2b) as their first priority. nevertheless, this strategy would never work without drivers, with that in mind, G7 aims to satisfy more than 9900 drivers and win their loyalty, considering them as a first-level customers.

In this perspective, my mission was taking the ownership of all solutions that help the driver in his day-to-day transportation activities, mainly mobile/tablet applications embeded with vehicle's hardware such as taximeter for example. The tablet application was already in production, and my responsibility was to take the solution ownership to develop and deploy new features requested by drivers, on the other hand, I designed from scratch the mobile application architecture, this app will be the driver companion when he's out of his vehicle, and will serve him to get notified about a customer's ride request in the area where the driver's vehicle is located.

Main responsibilities

  • Design and validate the system architecture for the new driver's mobile application integrating all stakeholders (dev team, business and marketing, ...)
  • Responsible of the overall system consistency and new features developement for embedded systems on the driver's tablet applications (eg. Analytics for fraud detection, dispatch service, ETA and routing service, ...)
  • Led technical and functionnal workshops to plan products' developement sprint according to business priorities
  • Analyzed driver's needs and software requirements to determine design feasibility taking system consistency into consideration.
  • Developed requirements for system modifications and new system installations.
  • Collaborated with the payment solution's vendor to evaluate eventual hardware impact on the payment processing on the driver's tablet application.
  • Performed beta-release software testing through testflight using a Mobile device manager.
  • Performed troubleshooting of post-release software faults to support live service.

Business impact

  • Better ride offer's acceptation rate meaning a better rider's and driver's overall experience
  • Software technical debt's decrease which contribute to build driver's loyalty
  • New business dashboards with real-time analytics running behind to empower better business decisions.
  • GA Release of driver's mobile app contributing to maintain a high ride offer's acceptation rate
